History

Tequila is well known as a Mexican spirit, traditionally made by cooking and fermenting agave. Originally created by the Aztecs in 250-300 A.D, it was used as ceremonial wine, “plaque” as a way to connect with their worshipped gods - Mayahuel (the goddess of the Maguey) and her husband Patecatl, the god of pulque. This drink caught on centuries later in the mid-1500’s and early 1600s, when the Spanish established a trade route between Manila and Mexico. The marquis of Altamira built the first large-scale distillery in what is now Tequila, Mexico.
